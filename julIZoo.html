Jul i Zoo

This was my very first project, and was aimed towards creating a microsite for Jul I Zoo that can inform and promote the event to the target audience. The website is made mobile first.
Jul I Zoo is an event for mostly families that celebrates Christmas with events, activities etc.

You can use the tabs below to see the different steps i had a part to play in.

Project management

This was my first time working with project management, and me and my team all decided that the best way to approach was to use Work Breakdown Structure to break down the whole project into simple tasks. The tasks were all estimated in days, if they had any dependencies, and when we would be able to begin them.

Thereafter they were placed into a Gantt-diagram which gave us a better overview of the project. This way we could always keep track of our process throughout the project, and if we were falling behind.

Information architecture

A part of the assignment was also to rearrange the information architecture on the page. This meant that i had to create a content inventory first - this content inventory was based on our user analysis.

Since a lot of our interviewee's focused on the need to see which events would be at Jul I Zoo we made a whole page just for this called events, which wasn't originally on the page.

Most of the interviewee's also mentioned that they were worried about the covid-19 situation. Therefore we made a page with information about precautions and how the Zoo put the costumers' safety first.

Below you can see the content inventory.

Prototyping

After i established the information architecture and the users' wants and needs through the user analysis, the prototyping phase begun.

The first iteration was drawn on paper to get a general idea of the placements and overall design.

The second iteration was made in adobe XD. Here i decided on an analog color palette that had colors the general public would consider Christmas colors; gold and red. We decided on this color palette based on our user analysis.

After the second iteration I did a think aloud test, to make sure the visitors attention and first impression were as desired. From this I made our third iteration.

The response from the test told us that the colorscheme and overall design was too boring, and for some of the interviewee's it felt more like a word document than a website. Therefore I changed the design and colorscheme quite a lot, and made smaller adjustments such as font sizes, moving around social media links etc.
